Literature Review 08.01

1. Digital twin paradigm: A systematic literature review

The fusion of the physical and virtual spaces is motivated to ensure a better flexibility and scalability of manufacturing systems through information technologies.
At the service stage, DT relies on real time state monitoring and virtual operations such as maintenance to predict the remaining life of components or products.

Frequent-asked questions:

  • Q1 What is a Digital Twin? (Digital Twin definition)
    A set of adaptive models that emulate the behaviour of a physical system in a virtual system getting real time data to update itself along its life cycle. The digital twin replicates the physical system to predict failures and opportunities for changing, to prescribe real time actions for optimizing and/or mitigating unexpected events observing and evaluating the operating profile system. 一组自适应模型，在虚拟系统中模拟物理系统的行为，获取实时数据以在其生命周期中自我更新。数字孪生复制物理系统以预测故障和更改机会，规定实时操作以优化和/或减轻意外事件，观察和评估操作配置文件系统
  • Q2 Where is appropriate to use a Digital Twin? (Application Contexts)
    • 1. Healthcare: Improving operational efficiency of healthcare operations
    • 2. Maritime and Shipping: Design customization
    • 3. Manufacturing: Product development and predictive manufacturing
    • 4. City Management: Modelling and simulation of smart cities
    • 5. Aerospace: Predictive analytics to foresee future aircraft problems
  • Q3 When has a Digital Twin to be developed? (Life Cycle Phases)
    • 1. In design phase
      • The digital twin is used to help designers to configure and validate more quickly the product development accurately interpreting the market demands and the customer preferences.
    • 2. In production phase
      • The digital twin shows a great potential in real-time process control and optimization, as well as accurate prediction.
    • 3. In service phase
      • The digital twin can monitor the health of a product, perform diagnosis and prognosis.
  • Q4 Why should a Digital Twin be used?
    • The digital twin can monitor the health of a product, perform diagnosis and prognosis.
  • Q5 How to design and implement a Digital Twin? (Architecture & Component)
    • The Physical layer involves various subsystems and sensory devices that collect data and working parameters.
    • The Network layer connects the physical to the virtual, sharing of data and information.
    • The Computing layer consists of virtual models emulating the corresponding physical entities.
  • Q6 What are the main challenges of implementing a Digital Twin? (Research Challenges)

Q1: Definition

A digital twin can be used to proactively identify potential issues with its real physical counterpart. It allows the prediction of the remaining useful life (RUL) of the physical twin by leveraging a combination of physics-based (physics from first principles) models and data-driven analytics. 数字孪生可用于主动识别其真实物理对应物的潜在问题。它允许通过利用基于物理（第一原理的物理）模型和数据驱动分析的组合来预测物理双胞胎的剩余使用寿命 (RUL)。

Here just specify some suitable definitions and keywords I think:
CPS （Cyper-physical System)

  • 1. A set of realistic product and production process models linking enormous amounts of data to fast simulation and allowing the early and efficient assessment of the consequences, performance, quality of the design decisions on products and production line.
  • 2. The virtual models include not only the geometric models, but also all rules and behaviours, such as material properties, mechanical analysis, health monitoring.
  • 3. The digital twin has been developed also for the waste electrical and electronic equipment recovery to support the manufacturing/remanufacturing operations.

Recycle

waste electrical and electronic equipment (WEEE) recovery: recycling at the material level and remanufacturing at the component level 材料层面的回收和组件层面的再制造

the collector needs a good understanding of the WEEE to evaluate the status and functionality before distributing them to the proper stakeholder. Similarly, the remanufacturer needs deep knowledge of the product components, before some of them are repaired or remanufactured. 收集者需要对 WEEE 有很好的了解，以便在将其分发给适当的利益相关者之前评估其状态和功能。同样，再制造商需要对产品组件有深入的了解，然后才能对其中的一些组件进行维修或再制造。
this paper aims to tackle the connectivity and integration issue along the WEEE recycling/remanufacturing chain

Challenges

  • DT Application Contexts
    • Human interaction is the one key challenge in the development and implementation of DT in the manufacturing application. 人机交互是 DT 在制造应用中开发和实施的关键挑战之一
  • DT Life Cycle & Functions
    • The ability to collect, aggregate and exchange data and information between different suppliers, manufacturers and customers could present interoperability issues. 在不同供应商、制造商和客户之间收集、汇总和交换数据和信息的能力可能会带来互操作性问题。
    • The development of standards-based interoperability is important and challenging at the same time for the evolution of digital twin applications along the entire life cycle. 基于标准的互操作性的开发对于数字孪生应用程序在整个生命周期中的发展来说既重要又具有挑战性。’
  • DT Architecture & Components/Technologies
    • The lack of an univocal reference architecture leads at developing Digital Twin solutions using different technologies, interfaces, and communication protocols, models and data 缺乏统一的参考架构导致使用不同的技术、接口和通信协议、模型和数据开发数字孪生解决方案
